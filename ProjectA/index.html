[THE CRISPICO]

Scientific Name:

Crispico "the wiggling creature"

Discovered by / at:

Dr.Dolor, Fifth Month of the 17th Year; Crepusculum

Family:

The only one ever discovered and the rest of it's species thought to be extinct. Belonging to the Psychrolutidae family, it has no bodily functions but it is suspected that it can breath, eat, and smell through it's skin like a toad.

Habitat:

Residing in the Crepusculum biome of the Twiligh Forest, it seems to be absorbing elemnents from the air and dumping it back out onto the ground. It is unknown if the Crispico is aware of its pollution, but it's actions cease during human interaction.

Appearance:

The crispico is an almost transparent-red blob with an outer layer of shiny black and white scales. The blob is very squishy.

Key Characteristics:

  • Tame.
  • Likes humans?
  • Squishy and Wobbly
  • Gets angry if you poke it too much

What we know so far about [ NAME OF YOUR CREATURE ]? The Crispico is a creature discovered in the Crepusculum biome of the Twilight Forest. The biome is named after its blue and purple lighting, of which creates streaks when the crispico interats with it. The creature is able to absorb nutrients and other things from the environment, depositing it back out after a bit of time. The origins of the creature are a mystery but local myths say it is the only remaining descendant of a powerful god, left behind from a civilization that is long-gone. If it's true, no one knows how its survived for so long. The objects that the crispico produces are physical but seem to vanish into thin air after some time. Although they pose no hard, sometimes there will be remains left and after a while they start spreading into other parts of the Twilight Forest. Upon first discovery, it seemed interested in the humans there, interacting with them and letting them poke it. It tends to reach towards you if you hold out your hand and will follow it like it's playing along. Interesting creature note: When it is playing with humans, it stops growing blocks. However, be careful; poke it too much and it will get angry, making the blocks poisonous.
Guidelines for human interaction with this creature Move your mouse to interact with the creature and some parts of it's environment that is close to it. When the mouse is moved to the main body, the generating blocks will stop.
